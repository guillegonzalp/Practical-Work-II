How is Kanye West one of the most influential artists in our generation.

Despite having started his music career as a mere producer, Kanye West, an artist raised up in Chicago,
with a middle-class background, has managed to become one of the most well-known and relevant music
artists today. Kanye, who has been a music enthusiast since he was young, began his career in the early
2000's producing for Roc-A-Fella Records, even producing albums for major artists such as Jay-Z and
earning gold and platinum records at a very young age.

However, when he wanted to quit being a producer to focus on creating his own songs, no record label
wanted to support him, in fact, they didn't want him to flourish in order to keep him as a producer, as
West was not only experimenting with a more cinematic and layered sound that had not been heard before
in hip-hop, but he was doing it as a complete outsider to the industry.

Kanye West managed to completely change the way certain tools were used in the music world, such as autotune, sampling
or interpreting. West also used Hip-Hop as a way to denounce everything he didn't think was correct, such as the
generalized homophobia in urban music, the racism in the United States and in musical organizations like the Grammys or
the lack of a father figure in most African-American families. It also helped numerous artists flourish, such as Tyler
the Creator, Travis Scott, Kid Cudi, Childish Gambino, Chance the Rapper and Metro Booming among others. And he managed
to receive preising from artists as big as Michael Jackson, Elton John, Paul McCartney, Prince and Bruce Springsteen.
